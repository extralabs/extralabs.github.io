BMP to PNG

 

  • BMP is a native image format in Windows. BMP supports RGB colors, color indexing, shades of gray, and the bit-oriented format. For a BMP image, you can use a Windows or OS/2® format, and a bit depth of up to 32 bits per channel. For 4 or 8 bit images compatible with Windows, you can also use the RLE compression method.
  • The PNG format (abbreviation of "Portable Network Graphics") was developed to replace another popular image format, GIF. On the one hand, PNG was meant to be a free alternative for GIF, whose creator patented it and, starting from 1995, demanded royalties from software developers who made applications for the Web. On the other hand, PNG was to overcome GIF's drawbacks related to its limited number of colors. PNG is not patented and can be used by anyone free of charge. PNG files can be recognized by the .PNG extension.
